Project Overview

This project analyzed 22,437 student records from the Open University Learning Analytics Dataset (OULAD) to investigate whether early engagement behaviors can predict academic success in online learning environments.

Using logistic regression and learning analytics techniques, I examined how Early Clicks and Early Active Days influence the likelihood of students successfully completing a course. The project demonstrates the application of educational data mining and predictive analytics to identify actionable indicators of student success.

One of the key findings was that Early Clicks emerged as the strongest predictor of student success, highlighting the importance of student interaction with learning materials during the first three weeks of a course.

Research Question

Can early engagement behaviors predict whether a student will successfully pass a course?

Key Findings

  • Students who passed their courses demonstrated substantially higher levels of early engagement than students who failed.
  • Early Clicks emerged as the strongest predictor of student success, indicating that students who interacted more frequently with course materials during the first 21 days were substantially more likely to pass.
  • Early Active Days also contributed to prediction accuracy, suggesting that both engagement intensity and engagement consistency are important indicators of academic success.
  • The probability of passing increased consistently as engagement levels increased.
  • Early engagement metrics can serve as useful indicators for identifying students who may benefit from timely academic support.
  • Learning analytics can provide data-driven insights to support student retention and success initiatives.
